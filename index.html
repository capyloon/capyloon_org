Why?

We believe a better Web is possible, for the benefit of all: users, content creators, service developers.

Modern mobile OSes like to present themselves as paragons of user friendliness and power. That can't be less true however: for instance, why can't I easily figure out what song was playing from a music service at a given point of a cycling route once home? Of course none of these apps can integrate with all the others - but the current OSes are not able to bridge the gap either.

The incumbents mobile OSes favor silos bundling apps with their data for technical and business reasons. Instead, you should to be able to make the most of the data you create and consume when using these services, on your own terms.

Capyloon is an experimental user agent that aims to give you what you deserve: being freed from the constraints imposed by a handful of OS vendors and service providers who decide what you can or can't do online, and how.

Fortunately, the Web as an application platform offers unique strengths to remedy to these problems. A web runtime has unrivaled capability to leverage your online activity for your benefit. That's why Capyloon is built as a Web based OS, re-using and extending b2gOS.

Because it's still early days and we don't have all the answers, it's important to keep an open mind and to experiment with new ideas. For instance, the current prototype includes early support for the IPFS protocol and for privacy respecting Web Assembly plugins. Can these technologies fit together to provide user control alongside the creation of an ecosystem for creators of content and plugins? What kind of payment system can fit in? What are the UX challenges?

Join Us

The code is available on github. The main repositories are:

  • Nutria : This is Capyloon set of web apps. If you are a front-end developer, you will find everything you need in this repository.
  • api-daemon : Non standard apis exposed to the UI. The contentmanager service is specific to Capyloon, and provides the data management layer.
  • B2G : Gonk based devices. The Pixel 3a and Android Generic System Images are currently supported.
